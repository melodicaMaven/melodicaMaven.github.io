<h2>Stuff I Do For Fun</h2>
<h3>Music</h3>
Primarily music performance. I have the most experience with oboe and flute, though I play around with the melodica, electric bass, guitar, ocarina, and piano. 
I would like to spend more time learning to record these instruments in a studio environment before I share any content, though.
My most notable works thus far are my arrangements, which are mainly ensembles and piano duets for a variety of video game tracks.
There are a couple of arrangements from other creators out there that inspired me to create piano duets specifically. Give these a listen; you won't regret it:

[Death by Glamour (from Undertale)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BcLBxtURD_g&list=RDBcLBxtURD_g&start_radio=1)

I share all of my completed arrangements on my <a href="https://www.youtube.com/@melodicamaven">YouTube channel</a>, though I always have a lot more in the works as I bounce between my other passions.

<h3>Raptor Conservation</h3>
I love all things scaley and feathered, so volunteering at an environmental center just made sense.
The local center has some un-releasable raptors who serve as educational ambassadors to the public and they are a joy to work with.
Apparently they don't get a lot of volunteers for the raptors since it involves handling dead mice and dirty work like cleaning out the mews.
If you're reading this and you love birds, check and see if your local aviary could use some additional hands.</p>

<h2>Stuff I Do For Work</h2>
<h3>Aviation</h3>
I am currently studying to become a commercial pilot.
The journey just to become a student pilot was long and arduous, and I will probably write about it on the blog at some point.
It's also very hard to learn to fly where I live considering the turbulent wind conditions, but I snag hours where I can between my other goings-ons and my day job.
<h3>Aforementioned Day Job</h3>
I currently work in customer service for a guitar manufacturer, which is a much more interesting job than it probably sounds on paper.
I started out in the woodshop as a guitar body sander. Pretty dull work, but it (and my current position) filled me with inspiration to get into woodworking.
I answer emails and calls about our guitars and other products, assist in resolving warranty claims, and most notably, I act as the customer service team's system admin.
The system administration is the part I enjoy the most, as I am not a particularly social creature. 
Much of my experience had actually come from the volunteer game project, as the system admin and project management components were highly transferrable to a lot of situations I've run into.
